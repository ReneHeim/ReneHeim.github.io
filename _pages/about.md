As a plant ecologist and remote sensing scientist, working at the interface of ecology, agriculture and plant pathology, I am curious to study plant–pathogen-environment interactions and how to best integrate remote sensing data into such studies. My motivation is to identify co-benefits at the interface of natural and cultivated landscapes that support both sustainable agriculture and resilient ecosystems. I have been trained in bioengineering [HAW Hamburg](https://www.haw-hamburg.de/bachelor-biotechnologie) and plant ecology [Universität Hamburg](https://www.biologie.uni-hamburg.de/en/einrichtungen/ipm.html), with research stays in [Valencia](https://www.uv.es/), [Sydney](https://www.mq.edu.au/), [Gainesville](https://www.eng.ufl.edu/), [Pretoria](https://www.up.ac.za/fabi) and [Ghent](https://www.ugent.be/bw/plants-and-crops/en). For more details on my research and to discuss collaboration and thesis supervision, please contact me (rene.heim@uni-goettingen.de).
